Read me first

How to actually use this thing.

Hey — Mickey here. Before you start clicking buttons, let me save you some time and give you the truth about how this tool works best.

🔁

This isn't a one-and-done thing.

Most parents think they'll run this once, get the magic words, and ride off into the sunset. That's not how kids work. That's not how relationships work. And that's not how this tool works.

Come back. Every time. Every blow-up. Every shutdown. Every weird moment where they said something that knocked the wind out of you. Every time you're standing outside their door wondering what to say. Every time they make a decision that scares you.

This tool gets better the more you use it. Your conversations get better the more you use it. Bookmark this page. Save it to your home screen. Make it the first thing you reach for when something happens — not the last.

📝

The more you tell me, the better I can help.

I get it — you're tired, you're upset, you want the answer fast. But the difference between a generic response and one that actually fits your kid is in the details.

When you fill out the snapshot, don't rush it. When you describe what's happening, don't summarize. Tell me:

  • Their world — friends, activities, recent changes, what's been weighing on them lately
  • The full scene — what was said, what was done, the tone, the timing, what came right before
  • The history — has this been building? Is this their first time saying something like this? Did something change recently?
  • Your part — what you said, how you reacted, what you wish you'd done differently

Vague in, vague out. Specific in, surgical out. That's the deal.

👨‍👩‍👧‍👦

Got more than one teen? Use it for all of them.

This tool isn't locked to one kid. If you have two, three, four teens under your roof — every one of them needs a different approach. Maybe one's a Shut Downer and one's a Reactor. Maybe one's a Performer and one's an Avoider. They aren't the same. And the words that work on one might be the worst possible thing to say to the other.

Run this tool separately for each kid. Take the Score Card for each one. Log conversations for each one. Build a separate library of what's working with each of them. Your kids deserve that level of specificity. So do you.

📓

Log every conversation. Even the ugly ones.

The journal isn't paperwork. It's how you start seeing what's working, what's not, and where you're growing. Especially log the ones that went sideways. Those teach you the most.
